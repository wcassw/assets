## Jeremiah 29:12–14
God promises to listen when His people pray and seek Him sincerely. When they search for Him with all their heart, He will be found and will restore them.

## 1 Thessalonians 5:17–18
Believers are called to live in constant prayer and give thanks in every situation. Gratitude and prayer are part of God’s will for those in Christ.

## 1 John 1:8
Anyone who claims to have no sin is deceiving themselves. Honest faith begins with admitting our need for God’s forgiveness.

## Galatians 2:20
The believer’s old life is crucified with Christ. A new life is lived by faith in Jesus, who loved us and gave Himself for us.

## Joshua 1:8
God commands His Word to stay in our mouth, mind, and actions. Meditating on Scripture and obeying it leads to spiritual strength and success.

## John 14:21

## Psalm 100:2–4
Worship God with joy, gladness, praise, and thanksgiving. Come before Him recognizing that He made us and that we belong to Him.

## Matthew 25:40

## Proverbs 3:5–6
Trust the Lord completely instead of relying only on your own understanding. Acknowledge Him in all areas of life, and He will guide your path.

# KJV Bible Verses: A Nation Born in One Day

## Key Verse

**Isaiah 66:8 — KJV**

> Who hath heard such a thing? who hath seen such things? Shall the earth be made to bring forth in one day? or shall a nation be born at once? for as soon as Zion travailed, she brought forth her children.

## Related Context

**Isaiah 66:7 — KJV**

> Before she travailed, she brought forth; before her pain came, she was delivered of a man child.

**Isaiah 66:9 — KJV**

> Shall I bring to the birth, and not cause to bring forth? saith the LORD: shall I cause to bring forth, and shut the womb? saith thy God.

## Summary

The main verse people refer to about Israel, or Zion, becoming a nation in one day is **Isaiah 66:8**. The passage uses the picture of sudden childbirth to describe Zion bringing forth her children quickly and unexpectedly.

The key phrase is:

> shall a nation be born at once?
